About

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Statistics at the University of Washington. I am broadly interested causal inference and causal discovery. Currently, I am working with Elena Erosheva and Carlos Cinelli to develop causal fairness and causal discovery methods with applications to the social sciences.
Before coming to UW, I worked as a data scientist at Marinus Analytics. Here, I leveraged advanced machine learning and time series analysis techniques to refine the processing of unstructured child welfare case records. Additionally, I pioneered the implementation of spam filters and underage person detection algorithms in TraffickJam, an application dedicated to supporting law enforcement in the crucial task of identifying trafficking victims and traffickers through the analysis of human trafficking advertisement data.
I received my Bachelor of Science degree in Statistics and Machine Learning from Carnegie Mellon University where I was fortunate to work with Peter Freeman and Alexandra Chouldechova.
